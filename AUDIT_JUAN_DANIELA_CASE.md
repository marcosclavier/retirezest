## Root Cause Analysis

### Problem Statement

The estate is growing at **$241,732/year on average** (5.75% annual return), which is **2.5x higher than withdrawals**. This creates a **$7.7M surplus** over 30 years instead of the expected gradual depletion.

### Contributing Factors

#### 1. TFSA Growth: +594% ($2.45M gain) ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Issue**: TFSA balances grow from $413k to $2.87M over 30 years.

**Why**:
- Tax-free compounding at ~7.2-7.4% annually
- **No TFSA contributions shown** in the data (odd given $14k/year contribution room)
- No TFSA withdrawals in first 30 years
- Allows massive compounding

**Evidence**:
- Year 1: $413,265
- Year 10: $773,325 (+87%)
- Year 20: $1,447,903 (+250%)
- Year 30: $2,866,067 (+594%)

**Calculation**:
```
Annual growth rate: (2,866,067 / 413,265)^(1/30) - 1 = 7.26%
Effective TFSA yield appears to be ~7.3% (above configured rates)
```

**Recommendation**:
‚úÖ Use TFSA withdrawals earlier in retirement (age 70-80) instead of letting it compound untouched for 30 years.

**Issue**: Non-registered accounts grow from $896k to $3.76M despite paying out distributions.

**Why**:
- Juan: 5% total return on investments
- Daniela: 6% total return on investments
- Distributions are paid out ($3.04M total over 30 years) but growth exceeds distributions

## Investment Growth Formula Analysis

Let's break down how the $7.25M growth occurred:

### Simplified Model

```
Starting Balance (SB) = $4,205,665
Annual Return (r) = 5.75% average
Annual Withdrawals (W) = $94,624 average
Annual Contributions (C) = $101,459 (NonReg dist) + $15,590 (CPP avg) + $10,561 (OAS avg) = $127,610
Annual Spending (S) = $204,840
Annual Tax (T) = $17,393
Years (n) = 30

Each year:
  Balance_new = Balance_old √ó (1 + r) - W + C - S - T
  Balance_new = Balance_old √ó 1.0575 - $94,624 + $127,610 - $204,840 - $17,393
  Balance_new = Balance_old √ó 1.0575 - $189,247

Net withdrawal rate: $189,247 / $4,205,665 = 4.5%

If r > net withdrawal rate:
  Portfolio grows

Actual: 5.75% > 4.5%, so portfolio grows at ~1.25% net per year

Over 30 years: $4,205,665 √ó (1.0125)^30 = $5,766,344
```

But actual ending balance is **$11,925,390** - more than double the simple model!

**Why?**

1. **Compounding on a growing base**: As portfolio grows, the 5.75% applies to larger balances each year
2. **TFSA untouched**: $413k compounding at 7.3% for 30 years = $3.4M alone
3. **Corporate account**: $2.44M growing at 5% with low withdrawals = $5.25M
4. **Distributions reinvested** (indirectly via spending surplus): ~$100k/year in passive income
